Synopsis

There is a current feeling of division in contemporary British society, with the generation gap feeling wider than ever. During evening Sunday television on BBC2, Mind the Gap (tm) will get two generations of the same family to compete together, testing their communication and teamwork skills, in order to bridge the generation gap. The show will reward teamwork with points rather than eliminating teams each round. The target audience is family viewers of every generation and we hope to encourage societal feelings of togetherness by showing the benefits of working together despite our differences. We are hoping to represent age, gender and cultural diversity through casting a variety of different families and diverse presenters. We believe as a programme for BBC2 that it should be family-friendly content which reflects the diversity of British society, given its responsibility as a public service broadcaster.

Format

6 hour-long episodes broadcast on BBC2 (8pm Sunday slot) over 6 weeks.

The programme will begin with the introdutory titles and the presenters will welcome the audience. A 30 second teaser of the show highlights will then show some of the most humerous content in the rest of the show, accompanied by the presenter narration which briefly introduces the format. The three families will then be introduced through footage of their home-life and clips from interviews. Each episode consists of three rounds. The round begins with the spin of a wheel which dictates the category of skill the teams will have to compete in. In the first round, the Junior contestant will try to complete 'Senior tasks' with verbal help and encouragement from their Senior, with the roles being swapped in round 2. Round 3 is the jeopardy round in which one contestant from each team will be presented an item from their team member's generation and have to describe what it is (as they are blindfolded) to help the team member guess what it is. The presenters will judge the communication and teamwork in each round and the family with the most points will win a weekend holiday somewhere in the UK.
